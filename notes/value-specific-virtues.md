Value-Specific Virtues

Excellence Within the Five Fundamental Domains

Introduction

While [[ideation/philosophy/virtues/meta-virtues-analysis-1|meta-virtues]] transcend all [[five-fundamental-values|five fundamental values]] and describe *how* to relate to any value domain, value-specific virtues describe excellence *within* a particular domain. These virtues use the language and concepts native to their domain and cannot be meaningfully applied elsewhere.

For example:

• **Loyalty** is specifically about Others—you cannot be "loyal" to truth or to your body in the same way

• **Vitality** is specifically Physical—you cannot have "vitality" in relationships or spiritual life in the same sense

• **Faith** is specifically Spiritual—you cannot have "faith" in your muscles or social networks in the same way

This document catalogs value-specific virtues organized by the five fundamental domains: Physical, Others, Self, Mental, and Spirit.

The Distinction: Value-Specific vs. Meta-Virtues

Value-Specific Virtues

• Describe excellence *within* a particular value domain

• Use the native language and concepts of that domain

• Cannot be meaningfully applied to other domains

• Represent what it means to excel at Physical, Others, Self, Mental, or Spirit

**Examples:**

• *Vitality* is Physical excellence—you can't have "vitality" in mathematics

• *Loyalty* is Others excellence—you can't be "loyal" to physical health

• *Faith* is Spirit excellence—you can't have "faith" in your muscles

Meta-Virtues

• Describe *how* to relate to *any* value domain

• Transcend specific domains

• Can be applied across all five fundamental values

• Represent qualities of skilled navigation of the framework

**Examples:**

• *Patience* applies to Physical healing, Others relationships, Self development, Mental learning, Spirit growth

• *Wisdom* applies to knowing when to rest (Physical), when to forgive (Others), when to assert (Self), when to speak truth (Mental), when to doubt beliefs (Spirit)

• *Courage* applies to facing physical vulnerability, confronting relational conflict, acknowledging shame, admitting ignorance, accepting meaninglessness

Conclusion

The complete map of human virtue includes both:

**1. Value-specific virtues** that define excellence within each fundamental domain

**2. Meta-virtues** that describe how to navigate across all domains

Together, these provide:

• **What matters** (the five fundamental values)

• **What excellence looks like in each domain** (value-specific virtues)

• **How to relate skillfully to all domains** (meta-virtues)

A fully integrated person demonstrates:

• Physical virtues: vitality, endurance, gracefulness

• Others virtues: loyalty, compassion, fairness

• Self virtues: dignity, authenticity, conscientiousness

• Mental virtues: clarity, creativity, competence

• Spirit virtues: faith, reverence, purpose

• Meta-virtues: patience, wisdom, courage applied across all domains

This is the complete architecture of human excellence.
