# Chapter Template: Field-Guide Engine

## Purpose

Create a consistent chapter form that carries argument, evidence, and emotional movement without flattening style.

## Standard Chapter Sequence

Use this section order in every chapter:

1. Field Note
2. Framework Claim
3. Evidence Triad
4. Steelman Rival
5. Practical Integration
6. Motif Echo

## Drafting Template

```markdown
## Field Note
[Open with a sensory natural-history scene tied to the chapter's value problem.]
[Keep concrete details first; interpret later.]

## Framework Claim
[State one falsifiable or contestable claim in plain language.]
[Name where it sits in the 5x3 map.]

## Evidence Triad
### Empirical Evidence
[Study, dataset, observed pattern, or historical data.]

### Philosophical Evidence
[Argument lineage, conceptual distinction, or logical support.]

### Lived Case
[A human-scale story with stakes and consequence.]

## Steelman Rival
[Present the strongest alternative interpretation.]
[State what the rival gets right before critique.]

## Practical Integration
[Give actionable implications for person, relationship, and institution.]
[Name likely failure mode and repair action.]

## Motif Echo
[Close with one primary motif line and one light echo.]
[Land a lived human turn, not a summary sentence.]
```

## Writing Constraints

- Theology may appear as comparative support, but the chapter must stand without theological assent.
- Do not let the opening scene become detached ornament; it must reappear in the argument.
- Keep one dominant claim per chapter; move secondary claims to side notes or adjacent chapters.
- Distinguish assertion, evidence, and speculation explicitly.

## Emotional Peak Rule

- Deliver one meaningful emotional turn per chapter.
- Rotate emotional function according to [[composition/march-18/motif-architecture-four-symbols]].
- Avoid consecutive chapters that end on the same emotional register.

## Chapter-Level QA Checklist

1. Clarity: can a general reader paraphrase the chapter claim in one sentence?
2. Evidence Balance: does each of the three evidence lanes materially support the claim?
3. Rival Integrity: is the rival presented in its strongest form?
4. Motif Function: does the motif advance meaning rather than decorate prose?
5. Closing Force: does the final paragraph create forward emotional momentum?
